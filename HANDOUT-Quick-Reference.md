## Congenital Heart Defects

### CYANOTIC (5 T's)
1. **T**etralogy of Fallot - Most common cyanotic CHD
2. **T**ransposition of Great Arteries - Needs PGE1!
3. **T**runcus Arteriosus
4. **T**ricuspid Atresia
5. **T**APVR (Total anomalous pulmonary venous return)

### ACYANOTIC
- **VSD** - Most common CHD overall (loud holosystolic murmur)
- **ASD** - Fixed split S2
- **PDA** - Continuous machine-like murmur
- **Coarctation of Aorta** - BP discrepancy, delayed fem pulses

## GI Emergencies

### Pyloric Stenosis (2-8 weeks)
- **Vomit**: Non-bilious, projectile
- **Exam**: Palpable "olive", visible peristaltic wave
- **Labs**: Hypochloremic metabolic alkalosis
- **Dx**: Ultrasound (pyloric muscle >3mm)
- **‚ö†Ô∏è CRITICAL**: Correct electrolytes BEFORE surgery!

### Intussusception (6-36 months)
- **Triad**: Colicky pain + Vomiting + "Currant jelly" stools
- **Dx**: Ultrasound ("target sign")
- **Tx**: Air/hydrostatic enema

### Malrotation with Volvulus
- **Bilious vomiting** = Think this first!
- **Dx**: Upper GI series
- **Tx**: Emergent surgery (Ladd procedure)

## Respiratory

### Asthma Exacerbation
**First-line**: Albuterol + Oral steroids
**Severe**: Continuous albuterol + IV magnesium + ICU

### Bronchiolitis (2024 Guidelines)
**DON'T**: Routine albuterol or steroids
**DO**: Supportive care (O2, suction, hydration)

## DKA (Pediatric Pearls)

**Key Difference from Adults**: CEREBRAL EDEMA RISK

**Management**:
1. Fluids: 20 mL/kg NS bolus, then SLOW rehydration over 48 hours
2. Insulin: 0.1 units/kg/hr (NO bolus in children)
3. When glucose <250: Continue insulin + add D5 to fluids

**Cerebral Edema Signs**: Headache, altered mental status, bradycardia
‚Üí Give mannitol, head CT, ICU

## Vaccination Highlights

**Live Vaccines** (contraindicated if immunocompromised):
- MMR, Varicella, Rotavirus

**Key Ages**:
- Birth: Hep B (within 24 hours)
- 12-15 mo: MMR #1, Varicella #1
- 11-12 yrs: Tdap, MCV4, HPV

**Rotavirus**: Must start by 14 weeks, finish by 8 months

## Child Abuse - TEN-4 Rule

**Bruising on**:
- **T**orso
- **E**ar
- **N**eck
- Child **<4** months

**OR any bruise in child <4 months = CONCERNING**

**High-Specificity Fractures**:
- Metaphyseal corner fractures
- Posterior rib fractures
- Multiple fractures at different healing stages

**Shaken Baby Syndrome**:
- Retinal hemorrhages + subdural hematoma + no external trauma

**Mandatory Reporting**: You don't need proof - just reasonable suspicion
